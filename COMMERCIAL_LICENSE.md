## What counts as commercial use?

Any use that is primarily intended for or directed toward **commercial advantage or monetary
compensation** is commercial use. The following are **explicitly commercial** and therefore
require a commercial license:

| Use case | Commercial? |
|---|---|
| Running OpenMonitor as a **paid hosted service** (monitoring-as-a-service, SaaS) | ✅ **Yes** |
| Offering OpenMonitor (or a derivative) to **paying customers** | ✅ **Yes** |
| Embedding OpenMonitor in a **commercial product or platform** | ✅ **Yes** |
| Using OpenMonitor to monitor infrastructure of a **for-profit business** | ✅ **Yes** |
| Using OpenMonitor to monitor infrastructure of a **non-profit / personal project** | ❌ No |
| Internal use at an educational institution or government body | ❌ No |
| Personal hobbyist or home-lab use | ❌ No |

> **Important:** Hosting OpenMonitor and charging customers for access to it — even
> indirectly (e.g., as part of a bundle or managed service) — **is commercial use** and
> requires a commercial license.

## Why a commercial license?

OpenMonitor is developed and maintained as an open-source project. The PolyForm
Noncommercial license ensures that the software remains freely available for personal,
educational, and non-profit use, while allowing the author to sustain development through
commercial licensing revenue.
